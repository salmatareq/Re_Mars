System Workflow



1

Regolith Collection & Processing

We start by collecting Martian regolith from the surface around the base. The soil is sieved to remove large rocks and impurities, then heated at 150–300°C to prepare it for water extraction.

2

Water Extraction

When the heated soil releases water vapor, it’s condensed into raw water. This water may still contain salts and dust that need to be filtered.

3

Primary Filtration

The raw water undergoes sedimentation or centrifugal separation to remove residues, then passes through sand and metallic mesh filters for initial purification.

4

Carbon-Based Purification

Carbon waste from oxygen-production experiments (like MOXIE) is converted into activated carbon. This carbon filters out toxic compounds and perchlorates through multilayer filtration (carbon, regolith sand, and fabric layers).

5

Final Treatment

The filtered water is distilled using solar or waste heat and optionally sterilized with UV light, making it safe for drinking, agriculture, or industrial use.

6

Fabric Recycling

Recycled fabrics from old clothes or cleaning materials are cleaned, sterilized, and added as filtering layers to support carbon and sand, preventing clogging and micro-particle leakage.

7

Filter Assembly

The full filter includes several layers: a fabric pre-filter, fine regolith sand, activated carbon, and a final nonwoven fabric layer — all enclosed in a transparent tube to visualize the flow process.

8

Smart Fabric Detection (AI Integration)

We can locate and classify usable fabric waste using our AI model, enabling automated sorting and collection of suitable textile materials for the filtration system.
